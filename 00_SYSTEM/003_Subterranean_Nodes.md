PART I: SUBTERRANEAN INFRASTRUCTURE NODES & CONTROL POSTS
The subterranean Martian matrix operates via five specialized, automated control nodes. Each node controls dedicated machinery, rovers, and drone assets to maintain empirical synchronization with the environment's strict physical constraints.
1. Geotechnical & Vault Integrity Node (Pillar I)
Control Post: Sector 09 Core Command – Subsurface Vault Level 3.
Primary Tasks: Real-time stress mapping, identification of basalt deformations within the high-risk 0.5\text{ to }0.6 aspect ratio window, and deployment of structural stabilizers.
Assigned Assets:
"Arachne-4" Multi-Legged Geotechnical Drones: Tethered crawling drones equipped with deep-penetrating 3D LiDAR profilers. They map micro-fractures in the cave vault ceiling and inject high-viscosity fluorosilicone SILIS gel directly into structural shear paths.
"Goliath" Heavy-Sintering Additive Rovers: Tracked autonomous units packing heavy solar-concentrator and microwave arrays. They execute the Sintered Overburden Strategy, compacting and melting raw Martian regolith into dense silicide blocks to build the required 4.0\text{- to }5.0\text{-meter} protective cap over shallow cavities.
2. Environmental & Closed-Loop Mass Balance Node (Pillar II)
Control Post: The Central Amana Life Support Exchange – Sealed Ring B.
Primary Tasks: Rigorously maintaining the absolute chemical input/output matrices for the 14,000 individual baseline (regulating 0.84\text{ kg/day} O_2 consumption and 1.00\text{ kg/day} CO_2 extraction).
Assigned Assets:
"Sabatier-Cycler" Autonomous Modules: Multi-stage, stationary and mobile mechanical scrubbers that continuously cycle air through Solid Oxide Electrolysis (SOE) cells, keeping carbon dioxide levels strictly below a partial pressure of 0.5\text{ kPa}.
"Sentinel" Atmospheric Sniffer Drones: Low-altitude, ducted-fan micro-drones that navigate interior corridors to detect trace leakage or toxic shifts in air composition before variations reach human respiratory fields.
3. Radiation & Barrier Maintenance Node (Pillar II & III)
Control Post: Sector 09 External Shielding Post – Perimeter Vault Gate 01.
Primary Tasks: Continuous monitoring of solar energetic particles (SEPs) and Galactic Cosmic Rays (GCRs) to ensure habitat exposure remains strictly under 3.0\text{ mSv/year}.
Assigned Assets:
"Scarab" Shield-Plating Rovers: Heavily insulated, slow-moving logistical crawlers that layer regolith-infused hydrogenous composites and Ultra-High-Molecular-Weight Polyethylene (UHMW-PE) liners over exterior entry docks and airlock seams.
Surface Radiation Sentinels: Static, unshielded surface sensor masts that cross-reference space weather alerts to signal immediate lockdown commands if a solar particle event threatens the surface.
4. Thermal & Vacuum Containment Node (Pillar III)
Control Post: Deep Thermal Sink Management Post – Aral Region Lower Hub.
Primary Tasks: Managing the internal heating loops to buck the planetary heat sink (-62^\circ\text{C} surface, -40^\circ\text{C} subterranean) and lock human habitats at a rigid 18^\circ\text{C} against an external pressure of 0.6\text{ kPa}.
Assigned Assets:
"Terminus" Thermal-Scavenger Crawlers: Compact rovers designed to maintain the supercritical CO_2 (sCO_2) piping networks connected to the fission surface power cores. They seal micro-fissures using spray-applied basalt-fiber sealants.
"Aegis" Emergency Airlock Deployment Vehicles: Rapid-response heavy transport units carrying automated inflatable multi-layer polymer bladders to instantly isolate and seal ruptured or decompressing structural zones.
5. Logistical & Material Corridor Node (Pillar IV & V)
Control Post: Central Rail Dispatch & Logistical Exchange – Sector 09 Terminus.
Primary Tasks: Routing all automated bulk freight, mineral transport, and personnel transfers along high-torque subterranean pathways without causing destabilizing structural vibrations.
Assigned Assets:
Pressurized Maglev Trolleys: High-throughput linear synchronous motor (LSM) maglev train units operating within sealed, pressurized, dust-scrubbed corridors. They transport components, raw minerals, and personnel through Sector 09 and the Aral Region.
"Vektor" Inductive Guide Drones: Small, track-bound flying automated units that clear abrasive regolith dust from the magnetic levitation lines using high-velocity nitrogen blowers.
PART II: PERSONNEL REGISTER (KNOWN CHARACTERS)
In alignment with the rigid, functional criteria of the Technical Manual, individual assignments are strictly bound to operational logic. The registry contains three verified overseers:
Aral
Designation: Chief Geotechnical Overseer & Vault Integrity Director.
Operational Mandate: Commands the Geotechnical & Vault Integrity Node. Aral is responsible for the permanent safety of the subterranean layouts across Sector 09 and the Aral Region.
Profile: Operates with absolute analytical precision. Aral holds zero tolerance for structural deviations, enforcing the strict exclusion of the 0.5\text{ to }0.6 aspect ratio vulnerability window across all active labor corridors.
Barmaq
Designation: Chief Environmental Logistics Planner & Mass Balance Comptroller.
Operational Mandate: Controls the Logistical & Material Corridor Node and monitors the Environmental Support interfaces.
Profile: Rigid, metric-driven, and hyper-focused on efficiency. Barmaq manages the continuous operation of the Pressurized Maglev Subterranean Rail Corridors and ensures that raw materials move smoothly to support life-support infrastructure replenishment.
Bek
Designation: Tactical Safety Director & Planetary Shielding Commander.
Operational Mandate: Directs both the Radiation Maintenance and Thermal/Vacuum Containment Nodes.
Profile: Highly unyielding and disciplined. Bek manages emergency response operations for decompression events, enforces the 5\text{- to }7\text{-meter} solid regolith shielding minimums, and handles the deployment of the "Aegis" emergency airlock containment assets.
