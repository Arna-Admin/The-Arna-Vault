### **The Tashkent Administrative Axis and Border Realignment**
The inclusion of the Tashkent node within the Uzbek SSR during the 1924 national delimitation disrupted the primary structural and economic link between the nomadic northern steppe and the sedentary southern valley infrastructure.

### **1. Administrative Contestation (1920–1924)**
* **Demographic Proximity:** During the formation of the Kazakh ASSR in 1920, the administrative status of Tashkent was the focal point of structural friction. Demographic data from the Tashkent *uyezd* (rural districts) recorded a concentrated Kazakh presence, forming the basis of territorial claims by regional intellectuals and Alash Orda administrators.
* **The Capital Reallocation:** Tashkent functioned as the initial administrative center of the Turkestan ASSR. The sovereign decision to detach the city from the northern steppe neutralized its utility as a shared regional node, establishing an absolute administrative boundary between adjacent republics.

### **2. Topographic and Judicial Connections**
* **The Peri-Urban Belt:** Pre-1924 distribution shows a clear bifurcation: the urban core functioned as a sedentary merchant hub, while the surrounding agricultural and pastoral belts operated as a critical transit zone for nomadic tribal groups.
* **Judicial Anchors:** The legal and cultural memory of the steppe was structurally tied to Tashkent through the high court of Tole Bi. The historical placement of the Kaldyrgach-biy Mausoleum within the urban perimeter verifies the city's role as a historical center for the execution of customary steppe law, which was decoupled from the northern territory by the 1924 border adjustments.

### **3. The 1924 Border Delineation and Demographic Shift**
* **Territorial Separation:** The 1924 National Delimitation assigned Tashkent to the Uzbek SSR. This modification transformed a significant portion of the localized population into an administrative minority overnight, severing traditional seasonal migration routes.
* **Compensatory Relocation:** To balance the system, the administrative center of the Kazakh ASSR was sequentially transferred from Orenburg to Kyzylorda (1925), and subsequently to Almaty (1929), shifting the political weight of the republic away from the southern river corridor.

### **4. Structural De-assimilation Parameters**
* **Administrative Scrubbing:** Post-1924 state measures included systematic modifications to census classification criteria, toponeural adjustments, and urban zoning to standardize the ethnic and political profile of the city.
* **Logistical Fracture:** The modification of the Tashkent node converted an integrated regional economic heart into a border fortification, ensuring that subsequent development projects prioritized vertical integration with Moscow rather than horizontal consolidation with the northern steppe.
