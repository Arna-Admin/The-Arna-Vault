### 1. HISTORICAL-MILITARY GRID: THE DASHNAQ STRUCTURAL OPERATIONS (1918–1920)
* **Operational Vector:** Deployment of armed detachments under the nominal authority of the Baku Soviet to execute the programmatic liquidation of the Turkestan Autonomy. 
* **Target Coordinates & Kinetic Footprint:**
  * **Kokand Node (Fergana Valley):** February 1918. Systematic execution and destruction of civilian infrastructure. Documented casualties: 10,000–35,000 neutralized units. This structural erasure served as the primary catalyst for the generation of the Basmachi asymmetric resistance network.
  * **Jalal-Abad Periphery (Suzak and Bazar-Korgon Nodes):** Coordinated Bolshevik-Dashnaq sweeps executed localized containment actions, forcing refugee flows toward regional margins.
  * **Transcaspian Extension (Ashgabat Sector):** Operational integration into regional security vectors, establishing a precedent of predatory liquidation tactics across the southern corridor.

### 2. SOVIET GEOPOLITICAL ENGINEERING: DELIMITATION PHASES (1924–1936)
* **Structural Intent:** Deliberate fragmentation of the organic, integrated urban-nomadic ecosystem ("Aral Family" axis) via administrative border manipulation to prevent the formation of a unified Turkic administrative center.
* **Chronological Matrix:**
  * **Phase I (1924):** Dissolution of the Turkestan ASSR. Allocation of regional assets to the newly delineated Uzbek SSR and Turkmen SSR. Relegation of Kazakh tribal territories into the misclassified "Kirghiz ASSR."
  * **Phase II (1925–1930):** Relocation of the Karakalpak Autonomy. Initially mapped to the Kazakh ASSR due to structural and economic linkages, it was reassigned directly under the Russian SFSR to block administrative concentration.
  * **Phase III (1936):** Final systemic stabilization. Handover of Karakalpakstan to the Uzbek SSR. The allocation functions as an intentional structural landmine to guarantee permanent friction over resource networks and water access corridors (Syr Darya / Amu Darya).

### 3. DECAPITATION OF THE AQ SUYEK LOGIC
* **Systemic Interdiction:** The imposition of external administrative boundaries permanently severed the traditional judicial corridors linking the Woral sector to the southern delta nodes.
* **Control Replacement:** The functional authority of tribal elders and sovereign judges (Bek lordship) was eliminated and replaced by localized NKVD operational cells.
