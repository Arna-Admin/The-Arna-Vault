The operational independence of the ministerial tier is neutralized via the systematic integration of primary family units into the state defense architecture, transforming dependents into institutional hostages.

### **1. Case Profile: The Belousov Node Leverage**
* **Target Isolation:** On **August 23, 2024**, the United States Department of the State applied blocking sanctions to the son of the Defense Minister (**Pavel Belousov**) and his spouse (**Yevgenia Belousova**) [2.2.2]. This action eliminated external asset extraction capabilities and jurisdictional migration routes.
* **Industrial Attachment:** Pavel Belousov’s corporate operations are linked to procurement pipelines feeding strategic propulsion manufacturing entities, including **NPO Energomash** [2.2.2]. This links the economic survival of the familial unit to the output of the strategic missile complex, rendering any production delay an administrative deviation punishable as sabotage.

### **2. Institutional Containment Framework**
The state enforces retention of elite dependents through explicit operational vectors:
* **Statist Anchor Roles:** Descendants of the command core are embedded into state-controlled commercial entities or defense-adjacent supply systems [2.3.1]. Any attempt to exit these positions or relocate outside national boundaries is classified under internal security protocols as desertion.
* **FSO Protective Surveillance:** The Federal Protective Service maintains continuous physical surveillance over the immediate family members of the primary *Cheget* authorization nodes. This deployment functions to verify physical location data, establishing a constant, accessible liability loop for the commanding officer.

### **3. Tactical Constraints Against Extraction**
* **Jurisdictional Lock:** The proliferation of multi-lateral enforcement frameworks and International Criminal Court directives restricts neutral-destination options (e.g., UAE, Turkey). Flight telemetry or unsanctioned transit requests trigger immediate interdiction.
* **Pre-emptive Defection Signal:** The structural removal of family components from monitored zones is recognized by internal security matrices as a precursor to administrative non-compliance. In the executive command structure, unauthorized familial relocation initiates the immediate suspension of command authority and triggers the physical liquidation of the primary actor.
