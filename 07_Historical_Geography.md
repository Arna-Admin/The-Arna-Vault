### **1. The Psychology of the End**
If the Sovereign perceives terminal defeat, the transition from military strategy to internal liquidation is a mechanical evolution. The **FSO** and **Rosgvardiya** act as internal containment units to secure the domestic front.
### **2. Central Asian Geography & Delimitation (1924–1936)**
Before 1924, territory was an ecosystem of the Bukhara Emirate, Khiva Khanate, and Kokand Khanate. The people around the Aral Sea—Kazakhs, Karakalpaks, and Uzbeks—shared a fluid structural relationship. Kokand served as the administrative and spiritual center for nomadic tribes along the Syr Darya. The **Aq Suyek** held the logic of the state from Kokand to **Oral**. 
The Soviets drew borders in three surgical phases to prevent a unified regional capital:
* **Phase I (1924):** National Delimitation. Dissolved Turkestan. Created Uzbek SSR and Turkmen SSR. Placed Kazakhs into the mistitled Kirghiz ASSR.
* **Phase II (1925-1930):** Karakalpakstan was shifted under the Russian SFSR to isolate it from Almaty.
* **Phase III (1936):** Formalized Kazakh SSR. Transferred Karakalpakstan to the Uzbek SSR as a long-term friction point over river systems.
### **3. The Geopolitical Anchor: Tashkent**
Tashkent served as the cultural, political, and economic lung for the steppe. During the **1920–1924** administrative tug-of-war, leaders like Alikhan Bokeikhanov argued its status based on the rural population density of Kazakh tribes. The city anchors the legacy of **Tole Bi**, who is buried there (Kaldyrgach-biy Mausoleum). Awarding Tashkent to the Uzbek SSR in 1924 cut the southern river civilizations from the northern depth.
### **4. The Nomadic Topographic Grid**
* **Kystau (Winter Node / Temirkazyk):** Sheltered river backwaters, Aral reed beds, and southern slopes of the Karatau. Stationary asset protection.
* **Zhailau (Summer Node / Urker):** High Saryarka plains, alpine meadows, and northern grasslands.
* **The Kos (Migration Corridors):** Structural meridian routes (South-to-North) extending up to 1,200 km.
* **The Voids (Betpak-Dala):** Protective barriers with controlled well coordinates used as tactical fortresses against outside intrusion.
